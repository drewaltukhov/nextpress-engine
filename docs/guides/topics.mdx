A **topic** is a tag you apply to a post. Each topic has its own archive page (`/topics/<slug>`) where every post tagged with it appears, newest first. Topics are how visitors browse your site by subject rather than by date.

This guide covers how to create topics, tag posts with them, and customize how topic archives look.

## When to use a topic

Topics work best for **broad subject areas** — the kind of thing you'd see in a site nav. A travel blog might have "Europe", "Asia", "Tips". A dev blog might have "Next.js", "React", "Performance".

If you need finer-grained labels (e.g. "Spain", "Italy" under "Europe"), use **pillars and spikes** instead — see [Pillars & spikes](/docs/guides/pillars-and-spikes).

## Creating a topic

1. **Topics** in the admin sidebar.
2. **New topic** at the top right.
3. Three fields:
   - **Name** — display name (e.g. "Next.js"). Shown as the topic chip on post cards and as the `<h1>` of the archive page.
   - **Slug** — URL segment. Auto-fills from the name.
   - **Description** — optional. Shown at the top of the archive page below the topic name.
4. Save.

The topic now lives at `/topics/<slug>` (the archive shows "no posts yet" until you tag something with it).

## Tagging posts with topics

From the post editor's right rail, open the **Topics** panel. It's a multi-select — pick zero or more topics. There's no hard limit on how many you assign, but two or three is the practical sweet spot.

You can also tag posts in bulk:

1. **Posts → All posts**.
2. Tick the posts you want.
3. **Bulk actions → Change topics → Pick topics → Apply**.

## Topic archives

Every topic has a public archive at `/topics/<slug>`. The default `single-pillar`-aware template renders:

1. The topic's **name** as the `<h1>`.
2. The topic's **description** (if set) under the heading.
3. A **list of posts** tagged with the topic, paginated (most-recent first).

The post list reuses the same `PostListView` widget the homepage uses, so layout, thumbnail visibility, and pagination style all match the site-wide display settings (Settings → Content → Homepage display options).

## Customizing the topic archive template

If your theme exposes a **Topic archive** template (most do), open the theme builder under **Themes → \<your theme\> → Open builder → Topic archive** to customize what shows on `/topics/<slug>`. Common tweaks:

- Add a hero image (Hero Title widget) above the post list.
- Add a custom right sidebar for topic archives.
- Insert an intro Rich Text block above the post list.
- Change the post list layout (grid vs. list) via the PostListView widget settings.

The change applies to every topic archive — there isn't per-topic template customization out of the box.

## Featured posts per topic

A common pattern: surface a "highlighted" post at the top of the topic archive. Two ways to do this:

1. **Pinning via topic order.** Posts in the archive sort by `published_at` desc by default. To pin a post, edit it and bump its **Published at** to a future date — it'll always be the first item in feeds. (Caveat: this also changes the visible date on the post card.)
2. **Pillars.** Convert the highlight post to a **pillar** for the topic. The pillar gets a distinct visual treatment on the archive and links to its spikes. See [Pillars & spikes](/docs/guides/pillars-and-spikes).

## Deleting a topic

From **Topics → All topics**, click the row's "•••" menu and pick **Delete**. NextPress checks how many posts are tagged with the topic before deleting:

- **Zero posts** — clean delete.
- **One or more posts** — you're asked to confirm. The posts stay; they just lose this one tag. Other tags on those posts are unaffected.

After delete, the archive URL returns 404. NextPress doesn't auto-redirect deleted topic URLs (rare enough that we'd rather make it explicit).

## Renaming a topic / changing its slug

From the topic edit page, change the name or slug, then save. If the slug changes, NextPress auto-creates a 301 redirect from `/topics/<old>` to `/topics/<new>` — see [Redirects](/docs/site-admin/redirects).

## Topic chips

By default, the post archive's post cards and the post-meta line on `/post-slug` show a small "chip" with the post's first topic. You can hide the chip per-instance via the widget's **Show topic** toggle (in the theme builder), or globally via the homepage display options.

## How the topic data flows

For curious users: topics live in the `topics` table; the join table `post_topics` connects posts to topics. Every public-facing query (topic archive, post-meta chip, sitemap entry) reads from that join. There's no taxonomy hierarchy — topics are flat. If you need a hierarchy, that's what pillars/spikes are for.

## Tips

- **Keep the list short.** Sites with 50+ topics dilute the value of every individual archive page. Two dozen broad topics is a healthier ceiling.
- **Slugs are URLs.** Pick slugs you can live with for years. Renaming is easy but the social shares pointing at the old URL won't update.
- **Topics show in the sitemap.** Every topic archive with at least one published post is listed in `/sitemap.xml` automatically — no extra step.
- **Don't reuse topic slugs as post slugs.** `/topics/foo` and `/foo` are separate routes, but the engine reserves slug words that conflict with the topic archive root (`topics`, `author`, `search`).
